Block 1 — Behavioral vs Demographic

Why most personas don't change any decisions

🔴 The Persona That Doesn't Do Anything

Marketing Mary — 38, CMO, urban, drinks lattes

Job: CMO · Age: 38 · Income: $150k · Hobbies: yoga

Mary wants to grow her business and loves data-driven insights. She's busy and values efficiency.

What decision does this persona change? None. It describes a stock character, not a customer. You could swap Mary for any persona and build the same product.
Most participants have seen this persona. Ask them to name a decision it ever changed. Silence is instructive.

✅ The Persona That Does

Dispatcher who hates re-routing

Role: Lead Dispatcher, 50–200 tech HVAC co. · Tenure: 10+ yrs in trades

  • Observed: Spends 45 min post-close reconciling tickets on paper (6 of 8 dispatchers observed)
  • Observed: Never uses keyboard shortcuts; refuses training that runs past 20 min
  • Reported: "Mid-day re-routes are my worst hour" (7 of 10 interviewed)
  • Inferred: Distrusts software that "hides" truck inventory behind a click
This persona changes decisions: no training longer than 20 min, inventory visible on primary screen, re-route flow is the hero path.
Highlight the evidence tags. This is where Day 1's evidence tiers earn their keep.

📚 Components of a Working Persona

🔍 Always include

  • Role + context (company type, size, tenure)
  • Observed behaviors (not beliefs)
  • Goals expressed in their words
  • Current workarounds and tools
  • Constraints (physical, organizational, cognitive)
  • Evidence tier per claim

❌ Usually skip

  • Stock photo + invented name (if it distracts)
  • Hobbies, lifestyle, brand preferences (unless load-bearing)
  • Traits written as adjectives ("busy," "tech-savvy")
  • Any claim you can't tie to evidence
Name + photo debate is a distraction — optional. Adjectives are the real killer because they're un-falsifiable.

Triads • 30 minutes

Your triad gets 3 real-world personas (sanitized from actual companies). For each:

  • Highlight every claim that's behavioral vs demographic vs adjectival
  • Mark which claims would change a product decision
  • Rewrite the weakest one in 10 minutes

One of the three provided personas is intentionally good. Make sure triads notice — not every persona is broken.

👥 Activity 2 — Build Your Persona

Triads • 45 minutes

Your triad has PR/FAQ customer quote + whatever assumptions you're carrying. Build a canvas-format persona for the primary customer in your product idea.

  • Start with what you actually know (provided research packet for FieldPulse)
  • Use AI only for Boxes 5 and 6 — and tag carefully
  • Circle the 3 claims you'd most want to validate with a real person
⏱ 10 min plan · 25 min draft · 10 min mark validation targets
FieldPulse research packet includes 3 de-identified dispatcher interviews and a ride-along note — enough grounding for a real draft.

Block 3 — Validation Interviews

Getting to evidence without leading the witness

🔔 The Five-Question Validation Protocol

  1. Walk me through the last time you did X. (Past behavior > hypothetical)
  2. What was hardest about that? (Let them name the pain)
  3. What did you do to work around it? (Workarounds reveal severity)
  4. How often does that happen? (Frequency calibrates impact)
  5. If you could wave a wand, what would change? (Only at the end, and discount heavily)
Do not ask: "Would you use a product that did X?" The answer is always yes. Observe past behavior instead.
This protocol is "Mom Test"-adjacent (Rob Fitzpatrick). Credit the lineage without requiring the book.

⚠️ Bias Traps in the Room

BiasWhat it sounds likeCorrection
Leading question"Wouldn't it be great if…?"Rephrase to past-tense behavior
ConfirmationOnly listening for "yes"Write down the 3 things that didn't fit your hypothesis
Single-storyN=1 generalized to a personaRequire 3+ interviews before claims
Enthusiasm"They loved it!"Count compliments vs purchase/use signals
Every bias here is common. Ask participants which they've caught themselves in — vulnerability speeds learning.

👥 Activity 3 — Role-Play Interviews

Triads (rotating roles) • 45 minutes

Each triad member takes a turn in all three roles: interviewer, interviewee (given a FieldPulse dispatcher character card), observer (tallies bias traps).

  • 10 min per rotation: 7 min interview + 3 min debrief
  • Observer uses a provided tally sheet (leading, confirmation, enthusiasm)
  • After three rounds: which validation target did not survive contact?
⏱ 30 min rotations · 15 min synthesize
The character cards are in the Day 3 activity folder. They include hidden details interviewers will only surface with good questions.

Block 4 — JTBD Stress-Test

Does this persona explain why they'd hire (or fire) your product?

🔨 Jobs to Be Done, Briefly

A Job to Be Done is the progress a customer is trying to make in a particular circumstance. Products are "hired" to make that progress and "fired" when something better comes along.

Form: "When I [circumstance], I want to [motivation], so I can [expected outcome]."

Example: "When a tech calls out sick mid-shift, I want to re-route the remaining jobs without re-keying them, so I can keep promised ETAs without staying late."
Keep JTBD light today — we're using it as a stress-test, not teaching a full framework.

🧪 The Stress-Test Checklist

  1. For each JTBD, can the persona produce a concrete last-time-this-happened story?
  2. Does the persona's current behavior explain what they "hire" today (even if it's a paper notebook)?
  3. If your product didn't exist, what would they do instead? Is that a credible fallback?
  4. What would have to be true for them to fire your product?
If the persona survives all four, you have something to engineer against. If not, you have an interview plan.
Question 4 is often the most uncomfortable — it's also the one that reveals brittle value propositions.

👥 Activity 4 — JTBD Cross-Examination

Paired triads • 60 minutes • Readouts

Triads pair. Each presents their persona + 2 JTBDs. The other triad plays "skeptical engineer" and runs the stress-test checklist out loud.

  • Every "I don't know" goes on a parking lot of open validation questions
  • Revise the persona to downgrade or remove unsupported claims
  • 60-second readout: one claim we upgraded, one we had to drop
⏱ 25 min per direction · 10 min full-room readouts
"Dropping a claim" publicly is the hardest thing we ask all week. Celebrate triads that do it honestly.
