# Week 1 — Customer-Centric Foundations

> *"A Technical Product Manager who cannot describe the customer's problem in the customer's own words is not yet managing a product."*

Week 1 grounds the Academy in the practice that separates TPMs from feature-delivery managers: **relentless customer understanding**. Every subsequent week — metrics, PRDs, architecture, stakeholder negotiation, delivery — loses anchoring without this foundation.

This week is the most group-oriented of the entire academy. Participants form **cohort triads** on Day 1 that persist through the end of Week 3 (carrying them into the mini-capstone).

## Learning outcomes

By Friday afternoon, each participant can:

1. Prompt a generative AI tool using structured patterns (role, context, constraints, format) to accelerate PM research — and know when *not* to trust the output.
2. Draft an Amazon-style PR/FAQ for a product idea and defend it against peer challenge.
3. Construct, validate, and stress-test a user persona from qualitative inputs — distinguishing observed behavior from assumed behavior.
4. Elicit and prioritize pain points using severity × frequency × addressability scoring.
5. Produce a **data-driven problem statement** that a reasonable engineering lead would accept as an input to scoping.

## Week 1 readout rubric (Friday afternoon)

Each triad presents a **10-minute integrated artifact**: persona → top 3 pain points → problem statement. Peer and instructor score on:

| Dimension | Weight | What "exemplary" looks like |
|-----------|--------|------------------------------|
| Customer authenticity | 25% | Persona grounded in observed behavior, not assumption; validation evidence named |
| Pain specificity | 25% | Pains are verbs ("cannot reconcile end-of-day inventory"), not categories ("bad UX") |
| Evidence quality | 20% | Every claim tagged with evidence tier (observed / reported / inferred / AI-generated) |
| Problem statement clarity | 20% | A senior engineer could scope from it without a clarifying call |
| AI-use transparency | 10% | Where AI was used, the prompt and validation step are disclosed |

## Bridge to Week 2

Week 2 opens by converting each triad's problem statement into **strategic design moves and metrics** — the North Star, operational signals, and customer-journey map that will turn Week 1's understanding into something measurable.
